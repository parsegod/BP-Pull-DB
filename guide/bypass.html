Tired of the Annoying "0/0" Bug? We've Got You Covered!

Are you a dedicated Call of Duty player on Xbox who frequently switches accounts, experiments with glitches, or just messes around in the game? Let's Get Started: Your Step-by-Step Guide

Ready to say goodbye to the 0/0 bug? Follow these simple steps:

  1. 1

    Load Up Call of Duty

    Start your game as you normally would and play until you reach a point where you need to switch accounts or change Player 1/Player 2 assignments.

  2. 2

    Spam "B" to the "Press A to Start" Screen

    This is crucial! When you're ready to switch, repeatedly press the "B" button until you return to the "Press A to Start" screen.

  3. 3

    Go to My Games & Apps

    From the "Press A to Start" screen, click your your xbox button and then click "My Games & Apps."

  4. 4

    Remove from Quick Resume

    Locate Call of Duty under "Quick Resume." Press the "Start button" (the one with three lines) and then select "Remove from Quick Resume."

  5. 5

    Game Closed!

    This action will completely close Call of Duty. That's it! By following these steps, 9 out of 10 times, you should circumvent your game from entering the 0/0 state.

How This Method Works: The Technical Bits

The effectiveness of this method hinges on the state in which you close Call of Duty.

The Critical "Press A to Start" Screen: If you close Call of Duty from any screen other than the "Press A to Start" screen, you have a 30x higher chance of glitching your save game into a corrupted state. Why? Because when you close the game from the "Press A to Start" screen, the game hasn't fully rendered in, even if you've already connected to online services. The moment you go back to that screen, you're essentially on a different, less data-intensive page. This means less data is being processed, allowing the game to close in a clean, graceful state.
